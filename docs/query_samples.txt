
If you're testing a SQL Agent on Chinook, don't just test simple SELECT queries. Test different levels of reasoning.

Level 1: Basic Retrieval

List all genres.
Show the first 10 tracks.
Find all albums by AC/DC.
How many tracks are in the database?
List all customers from Germany.
Show all employees who are sales support agents.

Level 2: Filtering

Which tracks are longer than 5 minutes?
Find customers from Canada.
Show albums released by artists whose name contains 'Queen'.
List tracks that cost more than $0.99.
Find invoices created in 2012.

Level 3: Aggregations

How many tracks belong to each genre?
Which genre contains the most tracks?
What is the average track length by genre?
Which artist has the most albums?
How many customers are there in each country?
What is the total revenue generated from invoices?

Level 4: Joins

Show all tracks along with their album names.
List album names and their artists.
Show customer names together with their support representative.
Display invoice information along with customer names.
List playlist names and the tracks they contain.

Level 5: Multi-Hop Reasoning

(These are the questions where many SQL agents start failing.)

Which artist has the highest total number of tracks?
Which customer spent the most money?
Which employee supports the largest number of customers?
Which genre generated the highest revenue?
Which album contains the most tracks?
Which country generated the most sales?
What are the top 5 selling artists?

Level 6: Time-Based Analytics

How many invoices were generated each year?
Which month had the highest sales?
Show revenue trends by year.
What was the average invoice value in 2013?
Which customer made the earliest purchase?

Level 7: Ranking Questions

Top 10 customers by spending.
Top 5 longest tracks.
Top 3 genres by revenue.
Top 10 albums by number of tracks.
Top artists by average track duration.

Level 8: Business Intelligence Style Questions

(These are excellent for testing a production SQL agent.)

Who are our most valuable customers?
Which genres should we promote based on revenue?
Which countries have the highest customer concentration?
Which artists generate the most sales?
What percentage of revenue comes from the top 10 customers?
Which playlists contain the most popular tracks?
What is the average revenue per customer?

Level 9: Hard SQL Agent Tests

(These require the agent to discover relationships across many tables.)

Find customers who purchased tracks from more than 5 different genres.
Which artist generated the highest revenue in a single invoice?
Find customers whose spending is above the average customer spending.
Which genre has the largest average invoice contribution?
Find artists that appear in the most playlists.
Which customer has purchased tracks from the greatest number of artists?
Which artists have tracks in every playlist?
Find albums where every track is longer than the album-wide average track length.

Level 10: Agentic Evaluation Set

(These are the types of questions I would use to benchmark a LangGraph/LangChain SQL Agent:)

What is the best-selling genre?
Which artist generated the most revenue?
Which employee manages the highest-value customers?
What are the top 3 genres in Germany?
Which customers have never made a purchase?
Which playlists contain tracks from more than 10 genres?
Find the artist whose albums have the highest average track length.
Show customers whose spending increased year-over-year.
Which genre has the greatest revenue per track?
What is the shortest path from customer to artist in the schema and use it to find the top artist purchased by each custom
